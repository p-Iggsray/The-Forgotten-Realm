About

Game Design & Development

Parker Riggs

The Forgotten Realm

✦

The Forgotten Realm is a proof-of-concept RPG built to show what it looks like when a large language model isn't a feature layered on top of a game — but the beating heart of it. Every NPC you meet generates their responses through a live LLM call. Conversations breathe. The world remembers who you are, what you've done, and how you speak to it. No two playthroughs are the same, because no two conversations ever are.

That comes with real tension. Language models inherit the biases, gaps, and tendencies of everything they were trained on. When you hand one narrative authority in a game, those qualities don't disappear — they become part of the story. The Forgotten Realm doesn't pretend otherwise. It puts the friction in plain sight, and asks whether something valuable can still emerge from an imperfect tool.

The broader argument this project makes is simple: AI in games doesn't have to mean procedural filler or dialogue trees dressed up with a chatbot. Used deliberately, an LLM can give a world genuine responsiveness — the sense that something on the other side is actually listening. Whether that makes games richer, stranger, or harder to trust is the question worth sitting with. The Forgotten Realm exists to make that question feel real.
